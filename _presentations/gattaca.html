Overview

  • Dystopian sci-fi movie from 1997
  • The first movie Andrew Niccol produced, wrote and directed
  • Starring Ethan Hawke, Jude Law and Uma Thurmann
  • Ended theatrical run with a revenue of only a third of its budget
  • Generally good reviews
  • Got an academy-award-nomination for "Best Art Direction"

Setting

  • Set in a near future
  • Technology allow eugenics and "perfect" children
  • "Valids" <-> "In-Valids"
  • Genoism is illegal but still practiced via genotype profiling
  • -> Discrimination

Plot

  • Naturally born and in-valid Vincent
  • He and his brother Anton regularly play chicken
  • Beats his "valid" brother and leaves home
  • Dreams of space-travel
  • Takes the place of disabled swimmer Jerome Eugene Morrow and works at Gattaca
  • A man gets killed at Gattaca
  • Eyelash makes invalid "Vincent" suspect in investigation
  • Nobody suspects valid Jerome Morrow
  • He barely evades the investigation several times
  • The director of Gattaca gets arrested
  • Anton was lead detective, learns the truth
  • Gets beaten and saved again in a game of chicken
  • Vincent goes on space mission
  • Dr. at Gattaca knew all along and makes him a valid
  • Romantic subplot
  • Falls in love with Irene
  • She eventually also figures it out
  • He looked over her flaws and so does she

Symbolism in names

  • GATTACA -> GTCA
  • Vincent Freeman
    • Vincent: invincible, latin: "to conquer, to win"
    • Freeman: selfexplaining
  • Jerome Eugene Morrow
    • Jerome: genome
    • Eugene: eugencis, greek: "well-born"
    • Morrow: tomorrow, future
  • Irene Cassini
    • Irene: greek: "peace"
    • Cassini: Giovanni Domenico Cassini, french-italian astronomer
  • Director Joseph
    • Joseph: Joseph Mengele, nazi eugenicist who conducted genetic experiments on Auschwitz prisoners
  • Detective Hugo
    • Hugo: germanic: "mind"

Fate

  • Vincent saves his brother
  • Real Jerome Morrow got second in a tournament and hit by a car
  • Irene is an exception with her fragile heart
  • Vincent goes to space in the end

Conclusion
